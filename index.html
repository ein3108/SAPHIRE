Welcome to Ubiquitous Accessible Home

Ubiquitous Accessible Home aims to provide an intelligent household that understands your voice command, gestures, and eye movements to open the door, turn down the heating or air-conditioning, change the lighting, start a vacuum, fill-up a bathtub, and more! This smart system will expand your control, seamlessly integrate your voice and motion commands with your house, making it smarter, cozier, and more efficient.

Project Overview

By utilizing Microsoft Kinect2 to analyze user gestures, Google Glass to understand voice command, and an eye-tracker to recognize eye movements, Ubiquitous Accessible Home presents a model house that comprehends qualitatively different multi-modal inputs to control many household sub-systems such as heating, air-conditioning, cleaning, and more. In comparsion with other systems, Ubiquitous Accessible Home allows user control of different forms to make a more accessible smart home. Users may lack the ability of giving commands in one or another form. For example, a sign-language speaker may or may not be able to give voice commands, or prefer using sign-language to any other form of commands. In this case, Ubiquitous Accessible Home's sign-language/gesture recognition system or eye movement tracking system can serve the user's needs properly. Ubiquitous Accessible Home's control system then sends the translated commands to a model house, executing different controls in the virtual model space. This virtual house model is our proof of concept.

Project Features

Ubiquitous Accessible Home provides three sub-translation systems that understand user's gestures, voice commands, and eye movement, respectively. It also provides a control hub system with a developer's API that can be utilized for injecting the translated commands into smart home interface such as Nest.
